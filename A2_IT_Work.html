What's Is a IT Professional | Definition | Tools | Meaning

https://www.educba.com/it-professional/

This page is a blog created by Priya Pedamkar, an IT professional with experience in software and database engineering, Eleading content writer at EDUCBA and 12+ years of experience in IT training. It explains that in layman’s terms, any person associated with the computer world is an IT professional. It gives six examples of IT professionals which are tech support, networker, internet and website designer, programmer, database developer and software developer, alongside brief descriptions of these professions. It details the relationship between an IT professional and the ever changing requirements of clients and customers and as such, an IT professional must keep up with the latest developments and technologies as well as other IT professionals. One of the biggest points of the blog is the challenge of communication; how and what to talk about with different types of people, explanation of problems and their solutions. It went on to give two different examples, one with a nontechnical person and the other with an entrepreneur, and their respective challenges; how the first should be kept simple while the second should be related more so to business.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qpZbRUEE_Dc

Using a ticketing system he is able to list and prioritise tasks, some of these tasks are assisting people with network issues , installing devices in client stores and although not normal for the job, writing scripts to help automate some tasks. Whilst also working on backup devices to keep a stores network running if the main one shuts down so they can recover it without their clients suffering any issues.

A DAY IN THE LIFE OF A DATABASE ADMINISTRATOR or DBA- YouTube

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EdBr-z2iFNA&ab_channel=ITCareerGuide

This video goes through what the role of a database administrator does. He mentions some popular software that is used by DBAs (Database Administrators), those being Microsoft SQL and Oracle, he displays in the video the decent salary (averaging from 71-120k+ depending on experience, location and more) of a DBA when they are specialized in Oracle because when dealing with Oracle, usually large databases are handled by those specialists. How to become a DBA, can’t graduate from college and go straight into DBA, instead much more experience and training with software and specialization is needed in order to transition into a more dedicated role. Daily routine of a DBA: check on systems for general issues and health, log files, servers and application portions of databases, certain transactions. Automation and using tools in this modern age is key to maintaining efficiency and effectiveness. Restoration, data backup and error prevention/troubleshooting are very important for DBAs.

What's It Like to Work in IT? The Scoop from IT Pros

https://www.comptia.org/career-change/why-change/it-pros-career-insights

This web source displays the results from interviewing 3 different IT professionals to gain some insight on what it's like to work in IT.
Bonnie Brennan- Angular Architect and Google Developer. She focuses on JavaScript development and architecture, with a focus on Angular and enterprise. She says that she became an expert through working with other developers, as a junior developer she was able to self-teach most of it, however, as a senior developer she stated that “there’s only so much you can learn on your own. I think it’s essential for senior developers to get involved in the community and learn from each other.” Her typical day at work as she has said is: “I work remotely, so I get up in the morning and go to my home office. I sit here and write code most of the day. I collaborate with other developers and do video chats. Most of the time I just sit here and write code.” The challenging aspect of her position: “Coding is hard to explain to someone unless they've gone through the process of using it and problem solved using it. When you're learning to code, you have to make mistakes so you're better at anticipating problems in the future.” Many mistakes are made in order to gain experience and be better prepared and grow in adapting to future situations.
Wendell Thacker- He is a network engineer who is currently working as technical support, help desk support and network engineering at a small company as at larger companies, he says that everything is sectioned off and as such there isn’t as much variety in their tasks. He is completing help desk tasks, like helping people reset passwords, and managing servers and setting up virtual machines. When working with other IT professionals, there is a running joke that people think that just because they are an IT professional, they can do anything. He says people can sometimes expect him to know how to use Photoshop, which he doesn’t as he is in network engineering and tech support. He says some necessary skills are, listening skills, communication skills, the ability to hold a conversation, and the ability to work well with all kinds of people. In his job, he is often working with and helping people, so it's better if you can build a rapport with them.
Ezequiel Pitty- Vice President of Infrastructure Engineering Core Services What he does: “I work mainly on server deployments and infrastructure management. In general, I provide maintenance and support to business applications.” He says that the skills needed for this role are: Independence, flexibility, the ability to be self-adaptive, the ability to work well under little or no supervision. Many of these skills suggest independence, it is unclear on whether he works with other IT professionals in his field of work. His typical day at work: “I configure scripts to perform server maintenance and functioning and load balancers and database deployments.” And how he spends most of his time: “My work life is between 40 to 60 hours per week, and I spend plenty of time with my wife and kids. I also take time off for vacation at least once or twice a year. My work–life balance is great.” Ezequiel does not mention any challenging aspects that he faces in his position, however, he does state that the personality that are a good fit for his position are: “Thinkers, people who use common sense and are able to react quickly to solve problems.” Potentially, if one does not fit this personality type, they could perhaps find difficulty in this position.
