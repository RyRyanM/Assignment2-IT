First of all, we have to know what the definition of cybersecurity is and what can it do? Cyber security may also be referred to as information technology security. It is the process and practice of protecting data,networks,programs,systems,devices and other information from unauthorized access,criminal use and security threat. Cybersecurity is very important to the world. Nowadays, many businesses and governments have a huge amount of top secret data stored in computers and it can transmit across networks to another computer. When talk about cybersecurity we can classified into 6 groups: Network-security, Data loss prevention (DLP), Cloud security, Antivirus/anti-malware, Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) or Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS) and last but not least Identity and Access Management (IAM) use.
Network security is a designed program or tool used by an enterprise to protect the integrity of your network and data.This program included both hardware and software. Secure networks help limit the ability to access, modify and encrypt data from other individuals or organizations. It stops them accessing and spreading your network. For example,Firewalls, antivirus scanning devices, and content filtering devices.
Cloud security means protecting data used for cloud computing systems and application from theft and deletion. There are many systems that can provide protection for cloud security such as firewalls, penetration testing, tokenization, virtual private networks (VPN), and avoiding public internet connections. Antivirus/anti-malware is a computer program that can be used to detect computer threats. It is also a system that can prevent,detect, and remove malware, and other malicious software like worms, trojans, adware, and more.Today’s more and more scam websites put many fake and attractive advertisements that contain viruses to harm our computer. Computers without antivirus/anti-malware software can slow down the device and cause it to crash.
An intrusion detection system is a device or software application that monitors a network or computer system for malicious activities or policy violations. Any detected activities or violations are typically reported to administrators or collected centrally by secure information systems and event management.
Data loss prevention(DLP) is a practice of securing and detecting potential data breaches, exfiltration,or unwanted destruction of critical information. Data loss prevention is very crucial to many small or big companies since data can reside on a variety of computing devices (physical servers, virtual servers, databases, file servers, PCs, and mobile devices) and move through a variety of network access points (wireline, wireless, VPNs, etc.),. That is why it can be facing some problems such as data leaks, loss...etc.

Nicholas - Robots

What does it do?

They can help in potentially dangerous situations such as helping fight forest fires, search-and-rescue missions after natural disasters, landmine detection in current or previous war zones or bomb/explosive defusal/ detonation.
They can be utilized for jobs which require high precision and accuracy. The previously stated bomb defusal is one such scenario but I can also be other objectives like, serving as a surgical assistant or locating and carrying items throughout a warehouse.
Another use of robots is in high effort tasks. Examples could include autonomous household robots which would carry out tasks like vacuuming or mowing the grass or working alongside humans in a manufacturing plant. They could even be used for companionship. With the improvements to artificial intelligence, a robot could offer companionship to elderly individuals or those in isolated situations.
The manufacturing industry is the most-well known user of robots or automated machinery. Robots can either work by themselves or alongside humans, at which point they are known as co-bots. to efficiently test and assemble products such as vehicles, phones, etc. It is estimated that the manufacturing industry utilizes more than three million industrial robots. The company Rethink Robotics, located in Boston, uses co-bots to help with handling materials, testing and inspecting products and parts and packaging finished products. It reportedly did the work of two humans. Another company from Boston, Universal Robots, created three different co-bots that were integrated into the automotive production line. By mimicking the movement of the human arm, it could bring added precision to different car making processes. It could replace human operators in jobs that could be dangerous, dirty or monotonous, allowing workers to perform more advanced tasks.
Most retailers and logistics companies are beginning to depend more on shipping, handling and quality control robots. In order to meet the increasing demand of customers for faster delivery, these companies have employed robots in warehouses and on roads to help maximize their time efficiency. Recently, Amazon has been using robots in their warehouses, to take items off the shelves, transport them across the warehouse floor and package them. Additionally, there has been a boom in the use of robots to deliver packages directly to doors also known as last-mile robots.
Robots have also made enormous strides in the healthcare industry. They can be seen in most aspects of healthcare, from robot-assisted surgeries to robots helping humans recover from injury in physical therapy. Examples of this include Toyota’s healthcare assistants, which help people regain the ability to walk, and “TUG”, a robot designed with a built-in map to navigate hospital halls in order to transport materials and supplies. It has been outfitted with the ability to scan it’s nearby environment and communicate with elevators, fire alarms and automatic doors via the hospital's Wi-Fi. Recently, pharmaceutical companies has employed robots in order to help speed up the fight against COVID-19. These robots are being used to fill and seal COVID-19 testing swabs are also being used by some in the industry to manufacture PPE and respirators.
Future development of robots include more humanoid robots and better artificial intelligence which can be trained either in a physical environment or simulated environment. The humanoid robot Brett from UC Berkeley lab taught itself to conquer a children’s puzzle using reinforcement learning, also known as trial and error. Though the process was slow, the robot taught it without input from a user. The hospital robot Tug can call for help if it’s roaming the halls and there are no humans around to move the object blocking it’s path.

Robots will change how we view certain matters. One set of questions could be, if a robot can be sent into a dangerous area, should it be sent in? What if a human could more accurately accomplish the same task? There will be more further questions but the main point is that we will need to re-evaluate many things we took as common sense. Where do we draw the line? What rights do they have? Robots will most likely not make most jobs redundant. Firstly, high-level jobs will most definitely not be made redundant as most require intelligence our current artificial intelligence are not possible for. Low-level jobs, though much easier to replace with robots will also most likely not be replaced by robots. The reason being, as unfortunate as it is, humans are much cheaper both in the short and long term for robots to replace. A factory which can run off just a bunch of people will most likely still run off those people. Not to mention slavery is still a thing in certain countries. Humans can also be more easily taught new things when compared to robots. Not only would a robot need new programming or training which humans are far better at, if the robot does not initially have the necessary support for the task, it would need to be re-designed. For example, I could learn to fold origami with the use of some instructions. A robot on the other hand would need to have a design which has fingers or finger-like appendages, then it would need to be programmed to fold the paper into shapes, and finally it would also need to be able to recognise the fact that there is paper in front of it. It is simply much easier for a human than a robot to learn new things.

Ryan - Machine Learning

What does it do?

Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence based on the idea that a system can learn from data, identifying patterns and making its own decisions without the need for human interference. Machine learning helps to automate many features, as well as lower costs and build efficiency as its ability to learn after each time it becomes more accurate. This makes large scale projects easier and more efficient. This is done by making the AI think in a similar way to a human, where it is able to learn and improve from last attempts. By looking at a task a set of rules is able to be set or a pattern is recognised to let the computer know what its own task is. Machine learning can be used for many applications such as fraud detection, product recommendation (e.g. amazon or spotify playlist) or even going through data, while people are able to go through data themselves the use of machine learning can speed up the process, especially with larger sets of data (for example going through spreadsheets) and this also lessens the workload for a user. What makes this so valuable is the ability for machines to pick up what our eyes might miss, as a machine sifting through data is able to recognise patterns that maybe be too complex or possibly overlooked by the human eye. In terms of programming language Python is the most widely used, although other languages can be used Python is considered the ‘best’ and ‘most popular’ (supported by github) due to its support in libraries to choose from. Machine learning can be done through different methods, a more commonly used method, ‘supervised learning’ as mentioned earlier, is a way of learning that is similar to humans where the data given is filtered to help it learn the basics of its specific task as it is able to generalise the data gathered, the machine is given set data referred to a training set which helps build the foundation of the task so the machine is able to understand the basic needs, over time becoming more accurate and getting more advanced. Supervised learning can often be classified models of data with binary “yes or no” solutions or regression data which deals with numbers. Another technique used for machine learning is ‘unsupervised learning’ the machine searches for patterns and the algorithm tries to learn the structure of data. This can be done by searching for similarities that have differences from other data so they can be grouped. Another method for unsupervised learning is dimension reduction, as the name suggests this method reduces the number of variables, once again grouping data with similar attributes. Other methods include semi-supervised learning, reinforcement learning, self learning, feature learning, sparse dictionary learning, anomaly detection, robot learning and many more, though supervised and unsupervised learning are the main types. The different methods are used for different applications based on their task, although many different methods can be similar and a combination of them can be used to solve certain solutions if needed. When training a machine learning model roughly 60% of datasets is used for the machines training, while roughly 20% is used to validate the results given by the machine after and the last 20% of data is used to check the machines data ensuring it is still accurate when presented new data.

What is the likely impact?

Machine learning can have many impacts, especially in discovery and improving efficiency and large scale projects. A popular usage of machine learning is in gaming, more known for board games such as Chess or Go, where machines have been taught to beat the worlds best players by competing against themselves millions of times to learn strategies and how to counter them. For the larger amount of the population machine learning will impact their daily life in aspects such as driving, as electric self driving cars are becoming more popular, although some people have some negative feelings towards this as they question its safety with other drivers and obstacles on the road, as well as the machines ability to read human error such as graffitied signs or signs that are hardly visible. Whilst people are still skeptical of self driving cars, Machine learning and AI can be used to store data on the environment such as weather which can help identify patterns and trends with the weather that would otherwise be missed by humans. This could impact all humans as we can identify weather patterns or natural disasters to prepare beforehand and most importantly to prevent climate change from human waste as much as possible. Machine learning is also growing in health care as AI is being used to assist in treating patients and developments are being made to assist with elderly care, ultimately improving healthcare, cost and efficiency.

Some examples of cloud services include Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive and many more.
